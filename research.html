Research

NOTE: THE LIST OF RESEARCH PROJECTS IS NOT FULLY UPDATED

The research within the DISTECH programme is organised into a collection of subprojects covering one or more of the core research areas. The portfolio of subpoject currently includes:

  • FORMGRID. The main objective of the FORMGRID project is to develop new techniques and software tools for diagrammatically modelling grid computing systems, and for formally verifying their correct behaviour. The groundbreaking character of the project is that the developed tools will allow users to model systems purely by constructing graphical diagrams, following the model-driven engineering philosophy, and that verification will be formal, exhaustive, and automatic.
  • Diagram Predicate Framework. In Model-Driven Engineering (MDE) models are the primary artefacts of the software development process. The usage of these models have resulted in the introduction of a variety of modelling languages and frameworks. Many of these languages and frameworks are based on the Object Management Group's Meta-Object Facility. In addition to their diagrammatic syntax, these languages use the Object Constraint Language to specify constraints that are difficult to specify diagrammatically.
  • The ASAP model checking platform. The goal of this project is to develop new state space exploration and model checking techniques for concurrent software systems. The project is concerned with research challenges related to the theoretical development of time- and space efficient algorithms and datastructures for state space exploration. The project also focuses on industrial application of the ASAP platform for verification of protocols for mobile networks and Internet-based services. The ASAP model checking platform is developed jointly with researchers at Aarhus University, Denmark.
